Intro

I am a research economist, at the European Central Bank since 2007.

I am also a CEPR Research Fellow, both in the Financial Economics Programme and in the Monetary Economics and Fluctuations Programme.

My areas of interest are:

  • finance, growth, and risk;
  • banks and the real economy;
  • finance and politics;
  • finance and labor.

My work has been published in top journals in both economics and finance, such as American Economic Journal: Macroeconomics, Journal of International Economics (2x), Journal of Development Economics, Journal of Financial Economics, Review of Financial Studies (2x), American Economic Review Papers & Proceedings, and European Economic Review.

My research papers have been presented in top academic conferences in economics, such as AEA (3x), AFA (2x), Darden International Finance (2x), ECB/CEPR Labor Market Workshop (2x), Econometric Society World Congress, EEA (3x), EFA (7x), FIRS (2x), Lenzerheide, RES (3x), SED, Utah Finance, and WFA (2x).

My research has been cited by the Economist, Bloomberg, Financial Times, and Frankfurter Allgemeine Zeitung, as well as received Best Paper Awards from the Review of Finance, the Australasian Conference on Banking and Finance, and the Cass Business School Emerging Markets Finance Conference.

I have taught an undergraduate course in econometrics at the University of Chicago and a graduate course in corporate finance theory at Tilburg University.

In the RePEc ranking, I am ranked in the top 1% in the world in the past 10 years, and in the top 100 in Europe.

Disclaimer: This is my personal website. The opinions expressed here do not represent the views of the ECB or the Eurosystem.

Recent

December 2021: The Economist featured my recent paper Carbon Taxes and the Geography of Fossil Lending with Luc Laeven to make the case for a global - as opposed to a piecemeal - carbon tax.

October 2021: I joined the Monetary Economics and Fluctuations Program and the Financial Economics Program of the CEPR as a Research Fellow.

May 2021: Political Cycles in Bank Lending to the Government (with Michael Koetter) was published in the Review of Financial Studies.

November 2020: My discussion of Orkun Saka's Domestic Banks As Lightning Rods? Home Bias and Information during the Eurozone Crisis was published in the Journal of Money, Credit, and Banking.

October 2020: Debt Overhang, Global Growth Opportunities, and Investment (with Francesca Barbiero and Marcin Wolski) was published in the Journal of Banking & Finance.

December 2019: On the Link Between the Volatility and Skewness of Growth (with Geert Bekaert) was published in the IMF Economic Review.

October 2019: The Invisible Hand of the Government: Moral Suasion During the Sovereign Debt Crisis (with Steven Ongena and Neeltje Van Horen) was published in the American Economic Journal: Macroeconomics.

June 2019: Credit Market Competition and the Gender Gap in Labor Force Participation: Evidence from Local Labor Markets (with Sonia Zaharia) was published in the European Economic Review.

June 2019: Do SMEs Benefit from Unconventional Monetary Policy and How? Micro-Evidence from the Eurozone (with Annalisa Ferrando and Gregory F. Udell) was published in the Journal of Money, Credit and Banking.

April 2019: I joined the Economic Policy Panel for the period 2019-2020.

February 2019: I joined the Journal of Banking & Finance as an associate editor.
